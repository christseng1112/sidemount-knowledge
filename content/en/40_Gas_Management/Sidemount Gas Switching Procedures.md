# Sidemount Gas Switching Procedures (Sidemount Gas Switching & Balancing SOP)

Because sidemount diving utilizes two independent cylinders, divers must manually switch breathing between the left and right regulators during the dive. This operation is necessary not only to maintain symmetrical buoyancy (preventing lateral roll) but also to **ensure that if one cylinder fails, the remaining cylinder holds the maximum possible gas volume** [1][2].

This article details the pressure balance protocol, the "Three-Check" standard operating procedure for regulator switches, and the safety protocols for technical deco gas switches.

---

## ⚖ Pressure Balance Protocol (Balance Pressure Protocol)

In dual-cylinder sidemount diving, the pressure difference between the left and right cylinders must be strictly managed.

*   **Maximum Pressure Offset**: The pressure difference between the two cylinders should **never exceed approximately 30–35 bar (approx. 500 psi)** at any point in the dive [1][3].
*   **Physical Reasons**:
    1.  *Trim & Balance*: Aluminum cylinders become positive as they empty. If the left cylinder is at 200 bar while the right is at 100 bar, the right tank will float up while the left sinks, causing the diver to roll toward the left side, causing fatigue [1][4].
    2.  *Redundancy*: If you breathe one cylinder completely dry before starting the other, a catastrophic failure of the full cylinder leaves you with zero gas, eliminating your redundancy backup [1][2].

### 🔄 Standard Switch Rotation Example (30-bar increments):
Assuming a start pressure of 200 bar in both cylinders, with the diver entering the water breathing from the right regulator (long hose):

```
[Start] Both cylinders at 200 bar ──> Breathe Right to 170 bar ──> Switch to Left
                                                                        │
                                                                        ▼
Breathe Right to 140 bar <── Switch to Right <── Breathe Left to 170 bar (Balanced)
  │
  ▼
Continue this 30-bar rotation cycle until reaching Turn Pressure.
```

## 🎯 The "Three-Check" Regulator Switch SOP

When switching regulators underwater, follow this standard sequence to prevent breathing from an empty cylinder, inhaling water, or triggering a regulator free-flow [3][5]:

```
Step 1: Check Pressure (SPG) ───> Step 2: Trace the Hose ───> Step 3: Purge & Swap
Read the SPG of the cylinder       Trace the hose from the        Purge the new regulator,
you are switching to [3].          valve to the mouthpiece.       swap it in, and stow the old one [5].
```

1.  **Step 1: Check Pressure (Check SPG)**:
    *   Before deploying the new second stage, read its pressure gauge (SPG) to verify that the cylinder contains sufficient gas. This prevents you from accidentally switching to a cylinder that is empty or has drained due to a leak [3][4].
2.  **Step 2: Trace the Hose (Identify Hose)**:
    *   **Critical Safety Step**: Trace the low-pressure hose with your hand starting from the cylinder first stage all the way to the second stage mouthpiece. This ensures that the regulator in your hand is connected to the cylinder you intend to breathe from, rather than a stage cylinder or your buddy's long hose [3][5].
3.  **Step 3: Purge & Swap (Purge & Swap)**:
    *   Spit out the old second stage (holding the new one in your hand). Point the mouthpiece of the new regulator downward and press the purge button briefly to clear any water.
    *   Insert the new regulator and take a slow breath.
    *   **Stow the Old Regulator**: Mount a left-side short hose back on its bungee necklace; clip a right-side long hose back to the right shoulder D-ring or secure it flat along your side. Verify that the stowed regulator is not free-flowing [3][5].

## 🧗 Technical Deco Gas Switch Protocols

During technical decompression diving where high-oxygen mixtures (e.g., Nitrox 50, Oxygen 100%) are used, **breathing a high-oxygen gas deeper than its Maximum Operating Depth (MOD) can trigger central nervous system (CNS) oxygen toxicity, leading to underwater convulsions and drowning**. Therefore, switching to a deco gas requires a strict "Team Verification Protocol" [6][7]:

1.  **Depth & MOD Verification**: Upon reaching the decompression depth (e.g., Nitrox 50 MOD is 21 meters at PO₂ 1.6), all team members establish a horizontal hover (Trim) and verify that their depths are stable on their computers.
2.  **Partner Cross-Check**:
    *   The diver unclips the deco cylinder, pushes it forward, and points to the gas label (e.g., "50" or "OXYGEN").
    *   The buddy looks at the cylinder label, verifies the diver's depth, and signals "OK" to confirm. **Never breathe from a decompression regulator until your buddy has confirmed the gas and depth** [6][7].
3.  **Trace the Hose**: The diver traces the regulator hose from the second stage back to the first stage on the deco cylinder.
4.  **Switch & Update Computer**: Purge the second stage, switch, and take a slow breath. Once breathing is stable, **manually update the active gas on your dive computer** so it calculates decompression obligation using the new gas mixture [7][8].
