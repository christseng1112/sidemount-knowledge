# No-Mount & Stage Dropping Techniques (No-mount & Stage Dropping Techniques)

In cave exploration and wreck interior penetration, divers frequently encounter spaces narrower or lower than their body profile with two mounted cylinders—"Major Restrictions." At this point, standard sidemount configuration cannot pass, and the diver must employ the most extreme sidemount techniques: **No-mount (complete cylinder removal)** and **Stage Dropping (temporary cylinder caching)** [1][2].

This article details No-mount restriction passage SOPs, safety risk management, Stage Dropping protocols, and post-drop single-sided trim correction.

---

## 🧗 1. No-Mount Restriction Passage Techniques

No-mount means **completely detaching all cylinders from the harness bungees and bolt snaps, and pushing them forward by hand** through an extreme restriction [1][2].

```
【No-mount Passage Workflow】
 1. Hover before restriction ──> 2. Detach cylinders ──> 3. Forward tandem push ──> 4. Shuttle through
    Establish neutral buoyancy      Unclip both bottom       Hold both cylinders by      Push cylinders through
    at the restriction entrance.    bolt snaps; release       the necks in tandem         first; body follows
                                    neck bungees [2].         (single-file) [2].          through the gap [3].
```

### 🟢 No-Mount SOP
1.  **Pre-Assessment**: Establish neutral buoyancy and hover at the restriction entrance. Confirm the passage dimensions exceed your body thickness and cylinder diameter [2].
2.  **Detach Cylinders**:
    *   Unclip the left and right bottom bolt snaps.
    *   Slide the left and right neck bungees off the valve handwheels. The cylinders are now completely detached from the harness [1][2].
3.  **Forward Tandem Push**:
    *   Grip both cylinder necks and first stages firmly.
    *   **Arrange both cylinders in a tandem line (one behind the other) along your body centerline** to minimize cross-sectional width [2][3].
4.  **Shuttle Through (Squeezing)**:
    *   Push the tandem cylinders into the restriction with arms extended.
    *   Use micro ankle kicks or pull yourself along the wall/guideline to "shuttle" your body through the gap [3][4].

---

### ⚠️ No-Mount Safety Risk Management
No-mount is an extreme technique with the highest life risk in technical diving. The following safety protocols are mandatory [2][4]:
*   **First Stage Impact Protection**: During the push, first stages are fully exposed. Your hands must **cup and shield the first stages and valve handwheels** to prevent impacts with the ceiling that could blow O-rings or accidentally close valves.
*   **Cylinder Loss Prevention**: In an unattached state, dropping a cylinder (e.g., in a vertical shaft or strong current) means instant loss of all gas supply. **Attach wrist lanyards (backup nylon loops)** to the valve necks after detaching—this ensures the cylinders remain within reach even if your grip slips [2][4].

## 📦 2. Stage Dropping Techniques (Temporary Cylinder Caching)

On long-range explorations, divers may detach carried stage cylinders and secure them to the main guideline to reduce drag, then retrieve them on the return leg [4][5].

```
                 [ Main Guideline ]
                           │
                      [Double-ended bolt snap]
                           │
              [Personal ID marker (Cookie / Marker)]
                           │
              [Dropped aluminum cylinder (valve up)]  <--- Secured to guideline
```

### 📋 Stage Dropping Safety Protocol
1.  **Physical Attachment (Line Attachment)**:
    *   Dropped cylinders must **never be placed loose on the floor**.
    *   Use a double-ended bolt snap to **lock the cylinder's top clip firmly to the main guideline**. This prevents the cylinder from being displaced by bubble wash, currents, or silt-out conditions [4][5][6].
2.  **Personal Identification Markers**:
    *   Attach a **non-directional personal marker (Cookie)** to the guideline at the drop point. This signals to subsequent dive teams that the cylinder has an active owner and belongs to a team currently inside [5][6].
3.  **Gas Calculation Safety Floor**:
    *   Before dropping a stage, confirm that your **remaining on-body gas is sufficient for continued exploration, the return swim, and full Rock Bottom reserves**.
    *   Never leave your Rock Bottom emergency gas in a dropped cylinder [4][6].
4.  **Silt Prevention**:
    *   When placing a cylinder, ensure the **first and second stages face upward** (away from the floor) to prevent silt infiltration.
    *   If silt enters a second stage mouthpiece, inhaling on retrieval can trigger a severe free-flow malfunction [6].

## ⚖️ 3. Single-Sided Trim Correction After Stage Drop

After dropping a stage cylinder, the diver's body transitions instantly from symmetrical loading to asymmetrical loading.
*   The pulling force from the dropped side disappears. The diver must release any compensatory ballast from the opposite side, or engage core muscles to maintain horizontal trim.
*   For detailed asymmetric trim correction techniques, refer to [[en/30_Skills/Single Cylinder Sidemount Configuration|Single Cylinder Sidemount Configuration]].
