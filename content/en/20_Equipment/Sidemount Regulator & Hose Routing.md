# Sidemount Regulator & Hose Routing

Because sidemount cylinders are suspended under the armpits, the orientation of the first stages and the hose routing are fundamentally different from traditional backmount configurations. An optimal hose configuration ensures **no dangling or excessively long hoses, keeps all hoses flush against the diver's profile, and facilitates smooth valve drills and long hose donations**.

This article uses the **Apeks DST** and **Scubapro MK25 EVO** as benchmarks to analyze the low-pressure and high-pressure hose routing for the left and right regulators.

---

## 🛠 First Stage Hardware Requirements

Sidemount regulators cannot be chosen at random. To maintain a streamlined profile, they must satisfy two key hardware requirements:

1.  **Swivel Turret**: The first stage must be equipped with a low-pressure swivel turret that rotates 360 degrees. This allows the low-pressure hoses to route toward the chest at natural angles, preventing hose crimping or pulling when the cylinders move [1][2].
2.  **5th Low-Pressure Port (Axial Port)**: Highly recommended. The 5th port is located at the top center of the swivel turret. This allows the BCD or drysuit inflator hose to route straight up parallel to the cylinder, eliminating interference with the primary regulator hoses [2][3].

---

## 📐 Left/Right Regulator Configurations & Routing List

The left and right first stages in a sidemount configuration are "mirror-symmetrical," but their hose lengths and functions are entirely different:

```
【Left Cylinder (Left Tank)】                     【Right Cylinder (Right Tank)】
  - Backup Regulator (Necklace, 56-61cm)        - Primary Regulator (Long Hose, 2.1m)
  - Drysuit Inflator Hose (5th Port, 35-45cm)   - BCD Inflator Hose (5th Port, 15-38cm)
  - SPG (Facing down, 15-20cm)                  - SPG (Facing down, 15-20cm)
```

### 1. Right-Side Regulator Setup (Right Side Setup)

The right cylinder serves as the diver's primary gas source and is used for gas donation in an emergency [1][4].

*   **Primary Regulator (Long Hose)**:
    *   **Hose Length**: **2.1 meters (7 ft / 210 cm)** rubber or braided hose.
    *   **Routing**: Exits from the side port of the right-side first stage swivel turret, runs vertically down the right cylinder body (secured with elastic hose retainers), loops back up, wraps around the back of the diver's neck, runs from the left shoulder across the chest, and enters the mouth. An **angled elbow adapter (90-degree or 120-degree)** is installed on the second stage inlet to align the hose parallel to the jaw [1][4][5].
*   **BCD Inflator Hose**:
    *   **Hose Length**: **Determined by the position of the BCD inflator valve**—a compact setup exiting from the 5th port directly to a nearby valve often uses **15 cm (6 inches)**; configurations requiring longer routing use **30–38 cm (12–15 inches)**, which is the mainstream sidemount LPI range [2][6][7].
    *   **Routing**: Connects to the **5th port** on the axial top of the first stage, running straight up along the back (or through the Razor harness guide) to the BCD inflator valve [2][6]. The axial outlet of the 5th port is what makes a short hose viable, as the hose runs parallel to the cylinder without bends.
*   **Right-Side Pressure Gauge (SPG)**:
    *   **Hose Length**: **15 cm to 20 cm (6 to 8 inches)** high-pressure hose.
    *   **Routing**: Connects to the high-pressure (HP) port on the first stage body, runs down along the cylinder, and is secured to the cylinder with an elastic loop or bungee, with the gauge face pointing downward (or toward the diver's field of view) for easy monitoring [2][4].

---

### 2. Left-Side Regulator Setup (Left Side Setup)

The left cylinder acts as the backup gas source and typically handles drysuit or secondary backup BCD inflation [1][4].

*   **Backup Regulator (Short Hose)**:
    *   **Hose Length**: **56 cm to 61 cm (22 to 24 inches)**. (Consistent with [[en/30_Skills/Second Stage Stowage, Switching & Retrieval|Second Stage Stowage, Switching & Retrieval]].)
    *   **Routing**: Exits from the side port of the left-side swivel turret, runs straight up over the left shoulder, and is mounted on a yellow **necklace bungee** positioned directly below the chin for instant access. A **120-degree elbow adapter** is recommended to optimize the angle of the mouthpiece [1][4][5].
*   **Drysuit Inflator Hose (or Backup BCD Inflator)**:
    *   **Hose Length**: **35 cm to 45 cm** (adjusted to fit the chest valve position).
    *   **Routing**: Connects to the left first stage **5th port**, running along the chest to the drysuit inflator valve [2][3].
*   **Left-Side Pressure Gauge (SPG)**:
    *   **Configuration**: Symmetrical to the right side, using a **15 cm to 20 cm** HP hose running down along the cylinder body and pointing downward [2][4].

## 📐 Advantages of Elbow Adapters

Installing a **90-degree or 120-degree stainless steel elbow adapter** at the second-stage hose connection is a vital detail in sidemount rigging:

1.  **Relieving Jaw Fatigue**: Without an elbow adapter, a 2.1m long hose or backup necklace hose pulls the second stage laterally or downward, forcing the diver to bite down hard to keep the regulator in place. This can lead to temporomandibular joint (TMJ) soreness. The elbow adapter allows the hose to align parallel to the cheek, significantly reducing leverage pull [1][5].
2.  **Preventing Hose Bowing (Avoiding Snags)**: Elbow adapters force hoses to run flush against the chest and shoulders, eliminating bowing hose loops that could catch on overhead wreckage or cave ceilings [5].

---

## 🎯 Hose Routing Characteristics Under the Razor 4.0 System

When utilizing the **[[en/20_Equipment/Razor Sidemount System Adjustment|Razor 4.0]]** system, pay close attention to the following routing details:

*   **Ultra-Short LPI Hose**: Because the Razor 4.0 BAT Wing 4 bladder sits extremely flat against the back, the BCD inflator hose exiting the right first stage 5th port must be measured precisely (a compact **15 cm / 6-inch** hose is often sufficient, up to a maximum of 30 cm depending on the inflator valve position [7]). The hose must run through the routing loops on the shoulder strap, flat against the back, to prevent any loops from bowing outward [2][6].
*   **Drysuit Hose Routing**: The drysuit hose exiting the left 5th port should run through the elastic loops on the waist belt before routing to the chest, preventing it from forming loose loops under the armpit [2].
