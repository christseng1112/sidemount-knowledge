# Fins and Mask Selection (Fins & Mask Selection for Sidemount)

Sidemount diving relies on precise propulsion (frog kick, back kick, helicopter turns) and a highly streamlined profile. The selection of fins and mask directly impacts the execution of these in-water skills. This guide compiles key selection principles and representative products.

---

## 🦶 Fin Selection Principles

Three core requirements for sidemount fins (for coordination with propulsion techniques, see [[en/30_Skills/Sidemount Propulsion Techniques|Sidemount Propulsion Techniques]]):

### 1. Short & Stiff Blade
*   A stiff blade provides **direct power transmission and crisp feedback** for frog kicks, back kicks, and helicopter turns, making micro-adjustments easy [1].
*   Long, soft fins (such as freediving long fins) can strike overhead ceilings or teammates in tight spaces, and make back-kicking nearly impossible.

### 2. Buoyancy Characteristics Matched to Your Suit
*   **Warm-Water Wetsuit Divers**: Choose fins that are **near-neutrally buoyant in freshwater**. Heavy rubber technical fins (like traditional Jet fins) will cause the lower body to sink, breaking horizontal Trim [1].
*   **Cold-Water Drysuit Divers**: Because drysuits trap air in the boots and make legs floaty, heavy rubber negative buoyancy fins help offset this lift to achieve a balanced Trim (see [[en/20_Equipment/Exposure Suit Selection & Trim Influence|Exposure Suit Selection & Trim Influence]]).

### 3. Spring Straps
*   Stainless steel spring straps allow for quick donning and doffing, eliminate failure points, and can be operated easily with thick gloves. They are the industry standard for technical diving [1].

## 🤿 Mask Selection Principles

### Advantages of Low-Volume Masks
1.  **Fast Clearing**: The small internal volume requires minimal gas and fewer exhalations to clear water [3].
2.  **Low Profile**: Hugs the face to minimize cross-sectional area and drag, matching the sidemount streamlining philosophy (see [[en/10_Concepts/Streamline & In-Water Balance Philosophy|Streamline & In-Water Balance Philosophy]]) [3].
3.  **Minimal Equalization**: Requires less gas to equalize the air pocket during descent, reducing mask squeeze risks [3].

### Practical Technical Diving Requirements
*   **Black Silicone Skirt**: Blocks lateral sunlight and reflections, keeping the contrast of torch beams sharp in caves or shipwrecks.
*   **Rugged Frame & Minimal Buckles**: Reduces mechanical failure points. Carrying a backup mask in a pocket (see [[en/20_Equipment/Accessories and Pocket Configuration|Accessories and Pocket Configuration]]) is standard protocol.
*   **Elastic Neoprene or Rubber Strap**: Prevents hair pulling and does not slip when worn over hoods.
