# Razor Sidemount System Adjustment (Razor Sidemount System Setup & Fitting)

The **Razor Sidemount System**, designed by technical and cave diving pioneer **Steve Bogaerts**, is the representative masterpiece of "Minimalist Sidemount." Evolving through versions 1.0, 2.0, and 2.5, the latest **Razor 4.0** system optimizes weight distribution and bladder fabrics, providing divers with a "Second Skin" experience in the water.

This article details the hardware components, harness adjustments, weight distribution, and trim methods for the **Razor 4.0 system**, with comparisons to the **XDEEP Stealth 2.0** at the end.

---

> 📘 This chapter has been updated and cross-checked against the **Official Razor 4 Side Mount System Manual (Edition 1.2, 2022-06-22)**, with official specifications noted as [11].

## ⚙ Razor 4.0 Core Components

```
                [ Rear Neck Delta ]
                  /            \
          [Left Strap]        [Right Strap]
            │   \              /   │
            │   [Delta Plate]     │  <--- Positioned between scapulae
            │        │            │
            │   [Spine Strap]     │
            │        │            │
          [Left Belt]─[Mini Plate]─[Right Belt] <--- MBP positioned at lower back
                     │
               [Crotch Strap]
```

1.  **Minimalist Harness**: Based on DIR/Hogarthian principles. The official setup consists of **two continuous webbing straps**—one 12ft (approx. 3.66 m) "shoulder/waist" resin-reinforced strap, and one 6ft (approx. 1.83 m) "lumbar/crotch" strap—linked through a **single closure point** at the **Delta Shoulder Plate (DSP)** and **Mini Backplate (MBP)**, eliminating bulk [1][11].
2.  **Razor 4 BAT Wing (Dual Redundant Wing)**: Features a **triple-layer Double Redundant Wing** structure. The primary bladder and backup bladder **each provide 45 lbs (approx. 20 kg) of lift** [11][12]. It integrates the base layer of the Pocket Weight System [11]. When inflated, it expands flatly to the sides and back rather than ballooning against the ribs, maintaining a streamlined profile [2][11]. ⚠️ **Important: The Razor 4 is a dual-bladder redundant configuration by default**, providing built-in backup buoyancy.
3.  **Side Mount Bungee & Bungee Tri-glide**: Features a **6ft continuous Side Mount Bungee** that crosses the back, passes under both armpits, and secures to shoulder D-rings. A Bungee Tri-glide (4-slot slider) is installed on the shoulder straps to micro-adjust tension, ensuring the cylinder valves hug the armpits [1][3][11].
4.  **Weight System: Pocket Weight System + Wing Weight Pockets**: The primary ballast is the **Razor 4 Pocket Weight System** (holds up to **14 kg / 31 lbs**) positioned along the body's **midline**. **Wing Weight Pockets** can be added for horizontal or lateral trim micro-adjustments [11][4].
5.  **Hardware**: DSP, MBP, 2× DAPs (Drop Attachment Points), Butt B-Ring, Wing Tab, Slotted BAT Wing Tri-glide, 2× 2" Bent Shoulder D-rings, 4× 1" Low Profile Waist D-rings, 1" Low Profile Scooter D-ring, and Wing Closing Tri-glide (all heavy-duty passivated stainless steel) [11].

## 🛠 Webbing Fit & Adjustment Steps (Razor 4.0)

Adjust the Razor system while **wearing the exposure suit (wetsuit or drysuit)** you intend to dive, preferably in front of a mirror or with a buddy's assistance [1][3].

### Step 1: Delta Shoulder Plate (DSP) Positioning
*   **Physical Location**: The DSP should sit on the spine, centered between the shoulder blades (scapulae), with its top edge roughly level with the armpits.
*   **Impact**: If the DSP is too high, the cylinder valves will hit the back of the head, limiting head movement; if too low, the cylinders will hang loose, causing a head-heavy Trim [1][3].

### Step 2: Mini Backplate (MBP) & Waist Belt Positioning
*   **Physical Location**: The MBP must sit on the lower back, just above the sacrum/hips.
*   **Waist Belt Tension**: Route the belt through the MBP, pull it tight, and secure it with the stainless buckle at the front. The belt must be tight enough to prevent the cylinders from rotating or shifting in the water, without restricting breathing [1].

### Step 3: Shoulder & Crotch Strap Adjustments
*   **Shoulder Strap Tension**: Shoulder straps run from the DSP over the chest to the waist belt. Adjust so that when standing with arms raised, the straps sit flat without pulling downward.
*   **Crotch Strap**: Runs from the bottom of the MBP, between the legs, and loops onto the waist buckle. **The crotch strap is the key to preventing the BCD from shifting upward when inflated**. It must be snug, but have enough play to allow crouching comfortably [1][3].

## ⚖/⚖ Ballast & Trim Micro-Adjustment (Razor 4.0)

The golden rule of sidemount is to align the **Center of Gravity (CoG)** with the **Center of Buoyancy (CoB)** [5]. Because exposure suits add lift, ballast is required, and its placement dictates Trim [5][6].

### 1. Official Buoyancy & Weight Check [11]
Official Step 9 weight check procedure:
1.  Enter the water wearing **full gear** (including suit and **full cylinders**).
2.  **Completely deflate** the BAT Wing (including the backup bladder) and vent all air from the drysuit.
3.  Stop kicking, hold a normal breath, and adjust weight until you **sink to eye level**. Exhaling fully should cause you to sink.
4.  Add a small amount of ballast to **compensate for the gas weight** that will be consumed during the dive.
5.  **Re-verify at the end of the dive** with cylinders at 50 bar / 750 psi.
*   **Note**: If the required ballast exceeds **6 kg / 13 lbs**, the manufacturer recommends using both the Pocket Weight System and the waist belt [11].

### 2. Trim Micro-Adjustment
Trim consists of two vectors: **horizontal (head-to-toe)** and **lateral (left-to-right)** [11].
*   **Horizontal Trim**: Sidemount often pulls the center of gravity down toward the legs. To correct a "foot-heavy" posture, **move weights upward** toward the top of the BAT Wing using Wing Weight Pockets. Move weights **downward** to correct a "head-heavy" posture [11].
*   **Lateral Trim**: Keep ballast close to the **body's midline** to minimize rolling torque. When diving a single cylinder, a single heavy stage, or a sidemount CCR, offset the roll by placing weights on the lighter side [11][6].

## 🔄 Side Mount Bungee Adjustments

The Razor 4 utilizes a **continuous 6ft Side Mount Bungee** running across the back, under both armpits, and securing to the shoulder D-rings. The cylinder valves are tucked under this bungee. Its tension dictates how closely the valves hug the armpits [1][3][11].

*   **Tensioning Principle**: Keep the bungee long initially and **shorten it progressively** after in-water tests. Crimp with hog rings and seal the ends once the optimal tension is found [11].
    *   **Too Tight**: Difficult to slide the valves under the bungee in the water; restricts shoulder movement.
    *   **Too Loose**: Valve handwheels flare outward ("sidemount dog ears"), increasing drag and snag risks [3][7].
*   **Bungee Tri-glide Adjustment**: Slide the 4-slot tri-glide on the shoulder straps to position the bungee exit point tightly behind the armpit, maximizing inward pull [1][3].
*   **Backup Bungee**: Bungees stretch and wear out over time. The manufacturer **strongly recommends** carrying a pre-measured spare Side Mount Bungee in your expandable pocket [11].

## 🛢 Cylinder & Suit Matching Guidelines [11]

Official recommendations based on environment:
*   **Steel Cylinders**: 7 / 8 / 10 / 12 L @ **240 bar** (LP steel tanks).
*   **Aluminum Cylinders**: 6 / 11 L @ **200 bar** (approx. AL40 / AL80).
*   **Cold-Water Drysuit**: Recommend **LP steel cylinders** as primary gas to offset drysuit buoyancy; **HP steel cylinders are not recommended** due to excessive negative weight.
*   **Warm-Water Wetsuit**: Recommend **aluminum cylinders** to prevent over-weighting.
*   **Stage/Deco cylinders must be aluminum**.
*   ⚠️ **Diving more than 2 cylinders requires specialized training** and falls outside the scope of the standard manual; consult a Razor instructor [11].

## ⚠️ Certification & Safety Declarations [11]

*   **CE Certification**: The Razor 4 BAT Wing is certified under **EN 1809+A1:2016** and **EN 250:2014**. **It is only certified for use with breathing air containing ≤ 21% (±2%) oxygen**. Higher oxygen mixes fall outside the CE certification scope.
*   **Original Configuration Only**: Any deviation from the manufacturer's layout (including the low-pressure inflator hose provided) voids the CE certification.
*   **BAT Wing is Not a Breathing Device**: Do not inhale gas from the BAT Wing bladders; do not use it as a salvage lift bag.
*   This manual **cannot replace hands-on training**; seek instruction from a certified Razor instructor.

## ⚖/⚖ Design Comparison: Razor 4.0 vs. XDEEP Stealth 2.0

These two systems represent the classic schools of sidemount design, with contrasting philosophies and configurations:

| Metric | Razor 4.0 (Minimalist / Continuous Harness) | XDEEP Stealth 2.0 (Modular / Heavy Duty) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Philosophy** | **Minimalism**. Low profile, fewer parts, acting as a "second skin" [1][8]. | **Modularity**. Offers Tec, Rec, and Classic versions to suit specific diving profiles [8][9]. |
| **Harness Structure** | **Continuous Webbing**. Uses two continuous straps (12ft shoulder/waist + 6ft lumbar/crotch) with a single closure point. Webbing adjusts at the DSP and MBP, providing a flat profile [1][8][11]. | **Independent Harness**. Shoulder straps and waist belt are separate; adjusting one does not affect the other, offering high fit tolerance [8][9]. |
| **Ballast System** | **Distributed Ballast**. Uses the Pocket Weight System and Wing pockets to distribute weights across three dimensions [2][4]. | **Central Weight Pocket (CWP)**. Concentrates ballast along the center of the spine, secured by Velcro and buckles, facilitating vertical trim [9][10]. |
| **Wing Attachment** | **Integrated Fit**. The wing is held tight against the back via the DSP, MBP, and waist bungees, preventing shifting [2]. | **Floating Attachment**. The wing is separate from the harness, looping directly onto the waist belt and crotch strap, allowing minor sliding in water [9]. |
| **Material & Protection** | Lightweight, low-drag, prioritizing flexibility and minimal bulk [8]. | Rugged protection. Harness padded at shoulders and waist; wing shell constructed of high-denier cordura [8][9]. |
| **OPV & LPI Position** | **Ambidextrous/Modular**. The LPI inflator elbow and the overpressure valve (OPV) ports are identical and interchangeable. **Default wetsuit setup is left-side BCD inflation, right-side lower dump** [11][12]; drysuit setup often reverses this to **right-side BCD inflation, left-side lower dump** to accommodate hoses [11]. | **Fixed Positions**. The LPI hose exits from the left side under the armpit and routes to the left shoulder strap; the OPV dump valve is located at the **bottom center of the wing**, reachable by either hand [3][9]. |
