# XDEEP Stealth 2.0 Configuration (XDEEP Stealth 2.0 Setup & Tuning)

The **XDEEP Stealth 2.0** is currently the most widely used and recognizable modular sidemount system in the global technical and recreational diving markets. Departing from the "integrated minimalism" of the Razor system, XDEEP utilizes a **highly modular design, independent shoulder and waist webbing, and a central spine ballast system** to offer ease of adjustment and heavy load capacity.

This article details the differences between the XDEEP Stealth 2.0 versions, core hardware design, fitting and adjustment steps, and practical cross-comparisons with the **[[en/20_Equipment/Razor Sidemount System Adjustment|Razor 4.0]]**.

---

> 📘 This chapter has been updated and cross-checked against the **Official XDEEP STEALTH 2.0 & STEALTH 2.0 TEC User Manuals (Revision 3, 2015)**, with official specifications noted as [13].

## 📐 The Three XDEEP Stealth 2.0 Versions

XDEEP offers three distinct wing configurations tailored to environment and cylinder materials:

```
【Classic Version】            【Tec Technical Version】       【Rec Recreational Version】
 (16 kg Buoyancy)               (19 kg Buoyancy)              (13 kg Buoyancy)
      ┌───┐                          ┌───┐                         ┌───┐
     /     \                        /     \                       │   │
    │  █  │                        │  █  │                       │  █  │  <--- Wing integrated with harness
     \     /                        /       \                      │   │
      └───┘                        └─────────┘                     └───┘
  Wing completely independent   Wide lower wing, ideal        Pinch buckles on shoulders,
                                for heavy steel tanks         easiest to don/doff
```

### 1. Stealth 2.0 Classic (Buoyancy: 16 kg / 35 lbs)
*   **Design**: The wing is completely separate from the harness, attached at the upper and lower ends using bungee cords and webbing loops.
*   **Best Suited For**: Divers using **aluminum cylinders (AL80)** or technical diving in warm tropical waters. Its flat, triangular shape maintains a streamlined profile, making it a favorite among cave divers [1][2].

### 2. Stealth 2.0 Tec (Buoyancy: 19 kg / 41 lbs)
*   **Design**: The lower portion of the wing is significantly wider to accommodate gas shifting, and the inflator elbow connects directly to the bladder.
*   **Best Suited For**: Technical divers using **heavy steel cylinders** or carrying multiple stage/decompression bottles. The wider lower chamber provides substantial lift at the hips, offsetting the heavy negative buoyancy of steel tanks [1][3].

### 3. Stealth 2.0 Rec (Buoyancy: 13 kg / 28 lbs)
*   **Design**: The wing is integrated with the harness and features **quick-release pinch buckles** on the shoulder straps.
*   **Best Suited For**: Recreational divers who do not enter overhead environments and dive standard single or double cylinders. This configuration offers the easiest and fastest donning and doffing [1][2].

> ⚠️ **Clarification on Dual Bladder / Redundancy**: The official manuals describe the Classic as a "double-shell" and the Tec/Rec as a "two-layer" structure—referring to the **outer Cordura shell + inner Nylon bladder, not dual bladders**. **Standard XDEEP Stealth 2.0 systems (including the Tec version) are single-bladder wings**. Divers requiring redundant buoyancy should verify the availability of a dual-bladder version, use a drysuit, or configure ditchable weight pockets (see [[en/50_Safety/BCD & Buoyancy Failure Emergency Procedures|Sidemount BCD Failure Handling]]).

> 🛟 **Official Safety Warnings**: The BCD is not a lifejacket and does not guarantee face-up floating on the surface. Never use the BCD as a salvage lift bag for objects heavier than 2 kg (use a dedicated lift bag). Nitrox mixes with oxygen concentrations above 23% fall outside the CE certification scope [13].

## ⚙ Core Components & Physical Properties

### 1. Independent Harness System
XDEEP's harness is constructed from three separate webbing segments, a significant innovation in sidemount design:
*   **Shoulder Straps (Soft Webbing)**: Softer webbing that conforms to the shoulders, reducing friction on wetsuits or drysuits.
*   **Waist Belt (Stiff Webbing)**: Stiff, resin-reinforced webbing designed to bear cylinder tension, ensuring D-rings do not slip under heavy loads.
*   **Crotch Strap (Medium Stiffness)**: Prevents the harness from sliding upward when the wing is inflated [1][4].
*   **Physical Advantage**: Adjusting the length of the shoulder straps has no effect on the position of the waist belt or hip D-rings, making harness fitting highly intuitive and localized [4][5].

### 2. Central Weight Pocket (CWP)
The CWP is the signature design feature of the Stealth 2.0:
*   **Configuration**: A vertical weight pocket running along the spine. It is secured to the spine strap using metal adapters, and **its height is adjustable**. While pre-configured at the top (for overhead diving), it can be moved downward to fine-tune horizontal Trim [13].
*   **Capacity Options**: S / M / L / XL sizes (approx. 4 / 8 / 12 / 16 kg capacity).
*   **Ditchable Waist Pockets**: The manufacturer offers optional bolt-on pockets (2×3 kg or 2×6 kg) that mount to the waist webbing using square D-rings, providing a ditchable weight option on the surface [13].
*   **Trim Pockets**: Small trim pockets can be mounted using tri-glides **almost anywhere** on the harness webbing to adjust weight distribution based on exposure suit buoyancy [13].
*   **Physical Effect**: Concentrating ballast along the body's midline provides excellent lateral stability, and adjusting the CWP height enables precise horizontal Trim control [4][6][7][13].

## 🛠 Webbing Fitting & Adjustment Steps (XDEEP Stealth 2.0)

Perform adjustments while wearing the exposure suit you intend to dive, following this sequence [4][7]:

### Step 1: Central Weight Pocket (CWP) Height Adjustment
*   The top of the CWP should align with the base of your neck (the C7 vertebra), distributing the weight across the thoracic and lumbar regions. If positioned too high, it will press against your neck; if too low, the ballast will gather at the hips, causing a foot-heavy Trim [4].

### Step 2: Shoulder & Waist Webbing Adjustments
*   Put the harness on and buckle the waist belt first. The **Square D-rings** on the waist belt should align precisely with the lateral midline of your hips (pelvic bones).
*   Tighten the shoulder straps. The chest D-rings should sit **approximately two finger-widths below the collarbone** (the optimal clip-in point for the upper bolt snaps) [4][7].

### Step 3: Wing Attachment & Hose Routing
*   **Classic/Tec Versions**: The top bungee of the wing wraps around the shoulder webbing intersection, while the bottom is secured to the waist belt using a stainless steel clip.
*   **LPI Inflator Hose**: The inflator hose exits from under the left armpit and is secured to the left shoulder strap via rubber rings.
*   **Overpressure Valve (OPV) Dump**: The dump valve on the Tec version is located at the bottom center of the wing. During a dive, it can be reached and vented easily with either hand, offering excellent ergonomic accessibility [3][4].

## 🔄 Practical Comparisons with the Razor 4.0

If you primarily dive the **[[en/20_Equipment/Razor Sidemount System Adjustment|Razor 4.0]]**, note these operational differences when diving the XDEEP system:

1.  **Weight System Differences**:
    *   *Razor 4.0* utilizes **Wing Weight Pockets** distributed around the perimeter of the wing, offering flexible lateral trim adjustments (useful for offset single-tank or rebreather diving) [8].
    *   *XDEEP* relies heavily on the **Central Weight Pocket (CWP)** along the spine. While this limits lateral weight shifting, it makes longitudinal Trim adjustments highly stable and intuitive for symmetric double-cylinder diving [4][6].
2.  **Bungee Configurations**:
    *   *Razor 4.0* uses a loop bungee with tri-glide adjustments to hold the cylinder valves tight under the armpits [8][11].
    *   *XDEEP* manuals outline **three bungee routing methods**: ① a single continuous bungee running behind the back; ② two independent bungees secured to the shoulder D-rings; ③ two independent bungees routing through a "floating loop." **The manufacturer notes that while method ① (continuous) is common, it pulls the D-rings forward, and recommends method ② or ③ instead** [13]. The system includes 10 meters of bungee cord for custom rigging.
3.  **Donning and Doffing**:
    *   The *Razor* continuous webbing lacks quick-release buckles; the diver must slide into the harness, but it maintains an extremely low profile in the water [8].
    *   The *XDEEP* harness features modular connections between shoulder and waist straps, making it easier to don and doff, particularly for divers with limited shoulder flexibility or when wearing thick drysuits [4][8].
