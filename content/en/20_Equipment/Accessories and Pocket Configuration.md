# Accessories and Pocket Configuration (Sidemount Accessories & Pocket Configuration)

Because a sidemount diver's lateral profile is occupied by cylinders, traditional thigh pockets on exposure suits become inaccessible and interfere with the close fit of the tanks. Consequently, sidemount diving has evolved a unique **accessory attachment and pocket stowage philosophy: all accessories must integrate into the body's streamlined profile, with zero dangling components, and critical safety gear must be accessible by either hand**.

This article details the configuration of the butt pouch, the positioning of cutting tools, the stowage of primary and backup lights, and the streamlined attachment of delayed surface marker buoys (DSMBs) and spools.

---

## 📂 The Butt Pouch (Utility Pouch) Configuration

The butt pouch serves as the diver's "mobile toolbox," primarily carrying spare gear that **does not require immediate emergency access** [1][2].

```
                 [ Butt Plate / Rear Waist D-rings ]
                      │                   │
               [Double Ender]      [Double Ender]
                ┌──────────────────────────────┐
                │      Butt Pouch (Pouch)      │  <--- Suspended at lower rear hips
                │  - Backup Mask / Wet Notes   │
                │  - Spare Bungees / Arrows    │
                └──────────────────────────────┘
```

*   **Attachment Point**: Suspended from the **butt plate D-rings or rear waist belt D-rings** using two stainless steel double-ended bolt snaps. The pouch should sit behind the hips in the dead space between the buttocks and upper thighs, which minimizes water resistance [1][2][3].
*   **Stowed Items**:
    *   Backup mask (low-profile folding design).
    *   Spare neck bungees (Spare Bungee) and spare bolt snaps.
    *   Wet notes.
    *   Cave/wreck line markers (directional arrows and non-directional cookies) [3][4].
*   **Physical Requirements**: The pouch must feature a flat, low-profile zippered design. It should stay flat when empty. Avoid over-packing the pouch, as bulkiness can cause entanglement in tight restrictions or create drag that causes the hips to sink [1][5].

## ✂ Cutting Tool Positioning (Cutting Tools)

In overhead environments or waters with discarded fishing lines, entanglement is a life-threatening hazard. Cutting tools must be configured for **ambidextrous access and redundancy**:

*   **Ambidextrous Access**: Cutting tools must be positioned where **either the left or right hand can draw them single-handedly**. If one arm is pinned by fishing line, the other hand must be able to deploy the tool immediately [2][5].
*   **Recommended Mounting Locations**:
    1.  **Waist Belt Front (Either side of the buckle)**: Mounting a line cutter or rescue knife here places it in the natural workspace where both hands can easily reach [2][5].
    2.  **Dive Computer Wrist Strap**: Mounting a compact line cutter directly to the wrist strap of your dive computer provides a highly accessible and intuitive draw point.
    3.  **Shoulder Harness Chest Area**: Securing medical shears or a titanium knife to the shoulder strap, held in place with rubber loops (Snoopy loops) to prevent slipping [2].

## 🔦 Primary & Backup Lights Configuration

### 1. Canister Primary Light
*   **Battery Canister**: Typically mounted on the right hip belt (adjacent to the buckle) or clipped horizontally to the lower back harness.
*   **Light Head**: Mounted on a Goodman handle worn on the **left hand**. This leaves the right hand free to operate inflators, clip cylinders, or share gas with a teammate [3][4].

### 2. Backup Lights - Standard Sidemount Stowage
Sidemount utilizes a standardized, highly streamlined method for mounting backup lights:
*   **Mounting Method**: Tie a bolt snap to the tail of the backup torch. Secure the torch **flat against the shoulder strap using two rubber rings (Snoopy loops or heavy-duty O-rings)** [3][5].
*   **Orientation**: The light head must point **downward** toward the waist, with the bolt snap clipped to the shoulder D-ring.
*   **Physical Benefits**:
    *   Pointing the light head downward prevents blinding teammates if the torch is turned on accidentally.
    *   The torch sits flat against the chest, eliminating drag and snag risks.
    *   If the primary light fails, the diver can locate, unclip, and deploy the backup torch single-handedly [3][5][6].

## 🎈 DSMB & Spool Attachment

*   **Delayed Surface Marker Buoy (DSMB)**: Folded flat and secured with bungee loops. Typically attached to the **lower centerline of the BCD wing** or clipped flat to the lower back harness. Never let the DSMB dangle loosely along your side [3][5].
*   **Spool**: Clipped to the left hip D-ring using a double-ended bolt snap. The spool must be held tight against the pelvic area to prevent it from swinging like a pendulum [3][5].

## 🎯 Accessories Under the Razor 4.0 System

When diving the **[[en/20_Equipment/Razor Sidemount System Adjustment|Razor 4.0]]** system, utilize its dedicated accessory components:

*   **Razor Utility Pouch**: An extremely flat pocket featuring internal elastic organizers. It attaches flat to the lower back harness via triangular buckles, providing excellent protection against impacts and loss in tight restrictions [2][10].
*   **Shoulder Backup Light Mounts**: The Razor system includes dedicated Snoopy loops to lock backup torches tight against the shoulder harness, exemplifying minimalist streamlining [10].
