# Sidemount Dive Log Template

Standard dive logs rarely capture the data most valuable for sidemount diving: **individual gas consumption per cylinder, gas rotation patterns, and specific rig alignment adjustments**. This template is designed specifically for sidemount—copy the block below to create new logs. The primary purposes of this log are to build your personal **RMV database** (see [[en/40_Gas_Management/Gas Consumption Rate (SAC & RMV) Calculation|Gas Consumption Rate (SAC & RMV) Calculation]]) and systematically lock down your gear configuration.

## 🧭 Execution Tips

1.  **The "One Change for Next Dive" field is the most important section of this log**. Adjust only one parameter at a time (e.g., bungee length, lead weight position, or cylinder band height) to isolate cause and effect. Once your configuration is optimized, this snapshot section will remain "same as above."
2.  **Accumulate at least 10 logged dives before averaging your RMV** for gas planning. Keep calm dives and high-workload dives (e.g., swimming against currents or task-loaded dives) separated in your calculations (refer to the task-load table in [[en/40_Gas_Management/Gas Consumption Rate (SAC & RMV) Calculation|Gas Consumption Rate (SAC & RMV) Calculation]]).
3.  **An end pressure offset exceeding 30 bar** indicates poor regulator switching discipline. Re-read [[en/40_Gas_Management/Sidemount Gas Switching Procedures|Sidemount Gas Switching Procedures]] to refresh your switching protocols.
4.  Export profile data from dive log software (e.g., Subsurface; see [[en/40_Gas_Management/Decompression Planning Software & Gradient Factors|Decompression Planning Software & Gradient Factors]]) to track your depth curve, and use this template to record **what dive computers cannot track**: specific gear adjustments, skill assessments, and notes.
