# Taiwan Sidemount Dive Sites Suitability Guide (Sidemount in Taiwan)

Taiwan offers year-round diving opportunities—the key is to **follow the seasonal monsoons**. When the northeast monsoon (typically from October to April) creates rough conditions in northern Taiwan and the Northeast Coast, southern regions like Kenting and Xiaoliuqiu remain open and calm [1][2]. This guide outlines Taiwan's primary diving regions from a sidemount perspective: entry methods, cylinder availability, water temperatures, and sidemount suitability.

## 🏖️ Regional Sidemount Details

### Northeast Coast (Longdong)
*   The primary training arena for northern Taiwan, located one hour's drive from Taipei [2]. The bay features terraced rocky ledges with 2–18m platforms ideal for skills training.
*   **The Sidemount Challenge**: Entering over wet, slippery tetrapods and rock paths with heavy gear. **The standard practice is to enter the water unencumbered, with cylinders handed down from the shore or clipped at the surface** (see [[en/30_Skills/Entry & Exit Techniques|Entry & Exit Techniques]]). Avoid carrying dual cylinders down rocky slopes.
*   Diving stops during the winter northeast monsoon. Requires 5mm wetsuits in summer and drysuits in winter (see [[en/20_Equipment/Exposure Suits Selection & Buoyancy Trim Impact|Exposure Suits Selection & Buoyancy Trim Impact]]). Abandoned fishing nets are common; carry redundant cutting tools (see [[en/50_Safety/Line Entanglement & Cutting Tool Configuration|Line Entanglement & Cutting Tool Configuration]]).

### Kenting (Hengchun)
*   Offers diverse dive sites, sloping sand flats, and straightforward shore entry routes [1]. Houbihu boat diving provides access to offshore pinnacle reefs. Sites on the west coast or Nanwan remain diveable during winter.
*   The Outflow (Chushuigou) and soft coral reefs are excellent sites for novice sidemount divers to practice buoyancy, trim, and propulsion.

### Xiaoliuqiu
*   **The only offshore island in Taiwan shielded from the northeast monsoon**, allowing year-round diving. Features a high density of green sea turtles [1][2].
*   Shore entries have concrete stairs, making gear carriage friendlier than the rocky terrain of Longdong. Note: local laws prohibit touching sea turtles; maintain excellent buoyancy.

### Green Island (Lyudao)
*   Widely regarded as Taiwan's premier dive site: features 20–40m visibility and landmark pinacles like Steel Reef and the Great Mushroom [1]. Primarily boat diving with giant-stride entries. **For boat entries, clip your cylinders before jumping or receive them at the surface**, coordinating protocols with the crew (see [[en/30_Skills/Entry & Exit Techniques|Entry & Exit Techniques]]).
*   Drift dive sites (such as the outer canyons) feature strong currents. **DSMB deployment is a mandatory skill** (see [[en/30_Skills/DSMB Deployment Techniques|DSMB Deployment Techniques]]).

### Orchid Island (Lanyu)
*   Features dramatic volcanic underwater topography [1]. The best season is March to September. The *Gyokuyo Maru* wreck in Baidai Bay is one of Taiwan's most famous wrecks; sidemount's streamlined profile is ideal for exploring around the structure (penetration discipline: see [[en/60_Advanced/Wreck Penetration Guideline & Reel Procedures|Wreck Penetration Guideline & Reel Procedures]]).
*   Remote island logistics are limited: **carry your own backup mask, regulator parts, and tool kits** (see [[en/70_Resources/Sidemount Gear Maintenance & Care Guide|Sidemount Gear Maintenance & Care Guide]]).

### Penghu
*   A summer destination featuring wreck clusters (e.g., the Jiangjun Wreck), basalt rock formations, and drift dives [1]. The region has large tidal ranges and strong currents; **plan conservative gas reserves** (see [[en/40_Gas_Management/Gas Planning & Safety Reserve Principles|Gas Planning & Safety Reserve Principles]]).

## 🛢️ Cylinder & Logistics Reality

1.  **Rental Cylinders**: Aluminum AL80 tanks (10.5–11.1L) are the absolute standard. Convertible DIN/Yoke valves are common. Sidemount divers should **carry left-valve adapters or verify the shop has modular left-valve tanks**. Left-valve cylinders are often limited; **specify "sidemount setup with left/right valves" when booking**.
2.  Dual cylinders are billed as two rentals. During peak season on offshore islands, cylinder availability can be tight; communicate your stage/deco needs early.
3.  **Nitrox Availability**: Major shops in Green Island, Xiaoliuqiu, and Kenting offer Nitrox, but check in advance during off-peak times (analysis discipline: see [[en/40_Gas_Management/Gas Analysis & Cylinder Labeling Practices|Gas Analysis & Cylinder Labeling Practices]]).
4.  For aluminum cylinder buoyancy characteristics (emptying tanks swinging positive) and ballast strategies, see [[en/20_Equipment/Steel vs. Aluminum Cylinder Buoyancy & Ballast Management|Steel vs. Aluminum Cylinder Buoyancy & Ballast Management]].
5.  For offshore island emergency evacuation routes, hyperbaric chamber locations, and DAN insurance coverage, see [[en/70_Resources/Diving Insurance & Emergency Evacuation Planning|Diving Insurance & Emergency Evacuation Planning]].
