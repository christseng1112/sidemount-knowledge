# Diving Insurance & Emergency Evacuation Planning (Dive Insurance & Emergency Evacuation Planning)

Technical sidemount diving is often conducted in **remote sites, deep decompression zones, or overhead environments**. In an emergency, the most expensive and time-critical aspect is typically not the medical treatment itself, but the **evacuation process to a hyperbaric facility** (involving helicopters, rescue boats, or international transfers). General travel insurance policies usually **exclude scuba diving or do not cover hyperbaric chamber treatments**. This article outlines the concepts of dive-specific insurance, the difference between DAN membership and insurance, and the preparation of an emergency evacuation chain.

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer**: Insurance terms, coverage limits, and regional availability change frequently. This article serves as a conceptual guide only. Before buying, read your policy terms carefully to verify that your specific diving profile (technical diving, CCR, or specific depth limits) is covered.

---

## 🧩 1. Critical Distinction: Membership vs. Insurance

Taking DAN (Divers Alert Network) as an example, membership and dive accident insurance are **two separate products** [1][2]:

| Parameter | DAN Membership | Dive Accident Insurance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Service** | 24-hour emergency hotline, medical consultations, evacuation **coordination** [2]. | **Covers actual medical expenses** (hyperbaric treatment, hospital fees, evacuation costs) [1][2]. |
| **Coverage** | **Does not cover medical treatment costs directly** [2]. | Covers treatment up to policy limits. |
| **Logic** | You join as a member first [1]. | **You add the insurance plan** to your membership [1]. |

> 📌 **Common Misconception**: Believing that "joining DAN" automatically covers medical bills. Membership covers **hotline support and coordination**; you must purchase a dive accident insurance plan to cover actual medical expenses [1][2].

---

## 💰 2. What Dive Accident Insurance Covers

A typical DAN dive accident insurance policy covers [1][2]:

*   **Hyperbaric Chamber Treatment**: The primary treatment for DCS/AGE. Single chamber runs can cost US$330–1,000 per hour, and multiple treatments are often required [2].
*   **Physician & Inpatient Fees**.
*   **Emergency Evacuation**: Transportation via ambulance, boat, or air ambulance to the **nearest appropriate medical facility or hyperbaric chamber** [1][2].
*   **Search & Rescue (SAR)** and accidental death or dismemberment benefits.
*   Certain policies cover **extra travel/lodging costs due to delayed return** and **loss of dive gear** (regulators, BCDs, dive computers) [1].
*   **Worldwide Coverage**: Crucial for dive travel to remote areas (Southeast Asia, Central/South America, the Caribbean) [1].

## 📞 3. The Emergency Evacuation Chain (Set Before the Dive)

Insurance is only a financial fallback; **field response procedures dictate survival**. Your pre-dive briefing should establish:

1.  **DAN Emergency Hotline Numbers**:
    *   DAN US 24hr: **+1-919-684-9111**.
    *   Save local regional hotlines (e.g., DAN Europe, DAN Asia-Pacific) to your phone and write them on your wet slate or shore emergency card.
2.  **Nearest Hyperbaric Chamber**: Check the status and contact info of the nearest active chamber **before departure**—do not wait until an accident occurs.
3.  **Emergency Oxygen Kit**: Keep a demand-valve oxygen kit on the boat or shore vehicle (see [[en/50_Safety/DCS Symptom Recognition & Oxygen First Aid|DCS Symptom Recognition & Oxygen First Aid]]).
4.  **Dive Profile Data**: Keep the casualty's dive computer ready for medical personnel, and record a timeline of symptom onset (essential for both DCS and SIPE; see [[en/50_Safety/Immersion Pulmonary Edema (IPE-SIPE) Recognition & Response|Immersion Pulmonary Edema (IPE-SIPE) Recognition & Response]]).
5.  **Policy Details**: Keep your policy number and emergency claim contacts written down or stored offline on your phone.

---

## 🎯 4. Considerations for Technical Sidemount Divers

1.  **Depth & Gas Limits**: Standard policies often have **strict depth or gas limits**. Technical diving, decompression stops, Trimix, CCR, and cave/wreck penetration usually require **purchasing a technical diving rider**. Verify that your policy explicitly covers your planned activity.
2.  **Gear Insurance is Separate**: Technical rigs (Razor, XDEEP) and CCR units are high-value items. Damage or loss is typically covered under a separate **in-water gear/equipment policy**, not your personal accident medical policy.
3.  **Annual vs. Short-Term**: If you dive regularly, an annual policy is more cost-effective. For occasional trips, consider short-term coverage, but verify it spans all travel dates and transfer locations.
