# Pre-Dive Checklists & Safety Drills (Pre-Dive Checklists & S-Drills)

Safe diving begins with a systematic pre-dive check on land and in the water. In sidemount diving, because you manage two independent cylinders, specific hose routing, and multiple clipping points, executing strict pre-dive checks and safety drills (S-Drills) is critical. These checks ensure that **both valves are fully open, hoses run without entanglement, and you can smoothly deploy the 2.1-meter long hose** in an out-of-gas (OOG) emergency [1][2].

This article details the sidemount-specific **GUE EDGE checklist**, the shallow-water **S-Drill and bubble check**, and the final "5-second checklist" before giant-striding into the water.

---

## 📋 1. The Sidemount GUE EDGE Checklist

GUE EDGE is widely recognized in technical diving as a highly structured team checklist. It is performed as a team on land or on the boat deck once all gear is fully donned [1][3]:

### 📧 E – Equipment
*   **Cylinder Attachment**: Verify that the left and right lower cylinder bolt snaps are securely clipped to your waist D-rings (or sliding D-rings). Ensure that the neck bungees are looped securely around the valve handwheels [4].
*   **Accessories & Pockets**: Ensure that your backup mask and line markers are stowed inside your butt pocket with the zippers closed. Cutting tools (shears and line cutters) must be mounted on the chest harness or waist belt where they can be **accessed single-handedly by either hand** [9][10].
*   **Backup Lights**: Confirm that the two backup lights mounted on your shoulder harness run flush, point downward, and have their bolt snaps clipped off [10].

### 🗺️ D – Dive Plan
*   Confirm the dive time limit, maximum target depth, and decompression schedule.
*   **Gas Planning**: Re-confirm the Turn Pressure (based on the 1/3 or 1/2 reserve rule) and Rock Bottom gas (e.g., a reserve limit of 40 bar in each cylinder) [3][6].

### 💨 G – Gas (Gas Analysis & Flow Check)
*   Confirm the analyzed oxygen percentage in both cylinders (to manage nitrogen narcosis and oxygen toxicity limits) and verify that the gas settings on your dive computer match [3][6].
*   **Valve Flow Check**: Have your buddy (or perform yourself) check both cylinder valves. **Confirm that both valves are turned counterclockwise until they stop (fully open, no turn-back)**. Never enter the water with a closed or partially closed valve. For the safety rationale behind "fully open, no turn-back," see [[en/50_Safety/Valve Drills Standard Operating Procedures|Valve Drills Standard Operating Procedures]] [4][8].

### 🛡️ E – Exposure & Extremities
*   Verify that your wetsuit is zipped or your drysuit zipper is closed. For drysuits, ensure the exhaust valve is open and the low-pressure inflator hose is connected and inflating smoothly [3].
*   Ensure your mask is in place and your fin straps are tightened.

## 🚨 2. In-Water Safety Drills: S-Drill & Bubble Check

The S-Drill (Safety Drill) and Bubble Check are conducted **immediately after entering the water at a shallow depth of 3 to 5 meters**, where buoyancy can be established away from currents and surge [10][12].

### 🟢 Step 1: The Bubble Check
1.  The team establishes a stable horizontal trim at 3 meters.
2.  Each diver slowly performs a 360-degree rotation.
3.  Teammates inspect the **left and right first stages, valve handwheels, low-pressure and high-pressure hose connections, SPG spool joints, and BCD bladder seams**.
4.  Verify that no bubbles are escaping. If any leak is detected, abort the dive immediately and return to the surface [10][12][15].

### 🔵 Step 2: Gas Sharing Safety Drill (S-Drill)
The S-Drill simulates an OOG emergency to build muscle memory for deploying the 2.1-meter long hose [8][12]:

```
1. Buddy signals "OOG" ──> 2. Donor deploys long hose ──> 3. Donor switches to backup ──> 4. Clear and extend 2.1m hose
   (Hand sweeps neck)          (Pass regulator to receiver)      (Switch to necklace reg)       (Clear chest of any snags)
```

1.  Your buddy signals "OOG" by sweeping a hand across their neck.
2.  You deliver the right-side long hose regulator directly into your buddy's mouth with your right hand. Your buddy begins breathing.
3.  You immediately duck your chin, locate the backup regulator on your necklace, purge it, and resume breathing [12].
4.  With your right hand, reach behind your neck to release the 2.1-meter long hose fully. Extend it to ensure **the hose runs free and is not caught on your light cord, camera, or BCD inflator** [4][16].
5.  Once your buddy is breathing comfortably and the hose is verified clear, conclude the drill. Recover the long hose, wrap it around your neck, stow the excess loop, switch back to the primary regulator, and signal "OK" [12].

## 🎯 3. The 5-Second Checklist (Before Giant Stride)

As you stand on the swim platform or boat edge, review these five items to prevent common pre-dive oversight errors:

1.  **V – Valves**: Are both my left and right valves open? (Verify by touching both knobs).
2.  **B – Bungees**: Are my neck bungees looped securely around my cylinder valves?
3.  **C – Clips**: Are both my lower cylinder bolt snaps clipped to my harness D-rings? Are the cylinders secure?
4.  **R – Regulators**: Am I breathing from an active regulator? Does the SPG needle remain steady as I inhale?
5.  **M – Mask**: Is my mask in place and adjusted?
