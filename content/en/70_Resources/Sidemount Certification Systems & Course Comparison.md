# Sidemount Certification Systems & Course Comparison (Sidemount Agencies & Courses Comparison)

Sidemount diving has become highly popular, and almost every major diving agency offers a dedicated sidemount course. However, these agencies differ significantly in their **teaching philosophy, gear standards, prerequisites, and in-water skill requirements**.

This article provides a systematic comparison of the sidemount training programs offered by **PADI, TDI, IANTD, GUE, and UTD** to help you choose the path that best aligns with your diving goals.

## 🧠 In-Depth Agency Philosophy Analysis

### 1. PADI (The Recreational Pioneer)
PADI's sidemount program is structured as a recreational specialty.
*   **Focus**: Focuses on recreational benefits (removing weight from the back on land, ease of clipping cylinders at the surface, and easy access to valves).
*   **Gear Requirements**: Flexible. Allows single-cylinder or dual-cylinder setups and places no strict restrictions on BCD styles (jacket conversions, American, or British-style rigs) [1].
*   **Pros & Cons**: Highly accessible with a low entry barrier. However, in-water trim, technical propulsion kicks (frog kick, back kick), and valve drills are taught to basic standards, which are generally insufficient on their own for cave or wreck penetration.

### 2. TDI (The Bridge to Technical Diving)
As one of the world's largest technical training agencies, TDI's sidemount course is designed as a rigorous foundation for technical diving.
*   **Focus**: Strong focus on core skills. Students must demonstrate stable horizontal trim hovering and master the frog kick, modified frog kick, back kick, and helicopter turn.
*   **Emergency Drills**: Includes high-load drills: valve shut-offs (Valve Drills), manual regulator feathering during free-flows, and long hose gas sharing ascents [2].
*   **Pros & Cons**: Highly practical and serves as the gold standard pathway to TDI Decompression Procedures, Cave, or Wreck training [2].

### 3. GUE (The DIR Standard)
GUE's sidemount course is a specialized tool within their system (which traditionally relies on backmount doubles). It is designed specifically for tight cave passages where backmount doubles cannot pass [3].
*   **Focus**: Absolute precision. GUE does not permit custom modifications; harness routing, regulator orientation, and hose lengths must match the GUE manual **exactly** [3][4].
*   **Team Orientation**: GUE views diving as a team-based sport. All gear and procedures are mirrored across team members so that anyone can operate a teammate's valves or deploy a long hose in total darkness [3][4].

## 🎯 Practical Advice: Instructor Experience Over Agency Logo

In sidemount diving, there is a widely accepted industry consensus: **"Choosing an instructor who actively uses sidemount for exploration is far more important than the agency logo on the card"** [1][5].

*   *Why*: Sidemount gear fitting is an art. It involves fine-tuning bungee length, ballast placement, and cylinder clip positioning.
*   *How to Evaluate an Instructor*:
    1.  Does the instructor actively dive in sidemount outside of teaching? If they primarily dive backmount and only wear sidemount to teach, they will struggle to help you optimize your rig [5].
    2.  Does the instructor dive the same system you own (e.g., your primary system, the [[en/20_Equipment/Razor Sidemount System Adjustment|Razor Sidemount System Adjustment]])? Matching gear profiles helps the instructor identify adjustments quickly [5].
