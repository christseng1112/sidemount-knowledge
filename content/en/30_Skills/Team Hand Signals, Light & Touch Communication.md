# Team Hand Signals, Light & Touch Communication (Team Hand, Light & Touch Communication)

Sidemount diving is frequently conducted in low-visibility, overhead, or tight restriction environments where hands are occupied (holding reels, lights, or cylinders). The communication system is split into three progressive levels: **hand signals $\rightarrow$ light signals $\rightarrow$ touch contact**, deployed based on visibility and distance. The golden rule of communication is: **every signal requires active confirmation**—a signal is not completed when it is sent, but when the recipient understands and confirms it [1][2].

---

## ✋ Technical Diving Hand Signals

### Differences from Recreational Signals
*   Technical signals are designed to be performed with **one hand**—the other hand is occupied holding a torch, camera, or reel [2].
*   Numerical signals (for reporting pressure) use a single-handed five-finger system: fingers extended vertically represent 1–5, horizontal finger configurations represent 6–9, and a circle represents 0. For example, reporting "150 bar" is signaled as 1, then 5, then 0.
*   When wearing three-finger mitts or dry gloves (in cold water), hand signals must be simplified and agreed upon during the pre-dive briefing [4].

### Core Hand Signals (Cave/Tech Standard) [1]

| Signal | Meaning | Remarks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **OK Circle** | Ask/Report status is OK | **Requires active response.** |
| **Fist** | Hold / Stop | All team members halt movement. |
| **Thumbs Up** | Abort Dive / Ascend | **Non-negotiable.** If anyone signals a thumbs-up, the team aborts. |
| **Index + Thumb Pistol Shape** | Out & Up—Exit Overhead / Retreat | Emergency exit signal [1]. |
| **Double-finger Figure-8** | Line Entanglement Warning | Alert buddy that they are entangled [1]. |
| **Hand Cutt-throat** | Out of Gas (OOG) | Triggers the regulator donation sequence (see [[en/50_Safety/Out of Gas (OOG) & Long Hose Sharing Procedure|Out of Gas (OOG) & Long Hose Sharing Procedure]]). |

## 🔦 Light Signals

When swimming along a guideline in single file, team members cannot see hand signals. Light signals become the primary tool for communication [1]:

| Light Beam Action | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Small Circle** drawn on nearby floor/wall | OK? / OK. (Requires a return circle to confirm) [1]. |
| **Slow Horizontal Sweep** | Attention—Non-urgent, look at me [1]. |
| **Rapid Vertical/Horizontal Flash** | **Emergency**—Immediate assistance required (e.g., OOG) [1]. |

*   **Rule of Lights**: **Aim the light beam onto a flat surface (floor or wall) in your buddy's field of view**, never shine the light directly into a teammate's face, which will ruin their night adaptation.
*   **Light Discipline**: Keep your beam steady in your scanning area. Erratic beam movement or a stationary beam is a passive signal that something is wrong, and team members should inspect the diver immediately.

## 🤝 Touch Contact

Touch contact is the final communication backup when visibility is zero (due to silt-out, mud, or total light failure). Both divers maintain contact with the guideline using a loop-finger grip, using their other hand to touch the teammate's arm [1]:

| Touch Signal | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Firm Squeeze** | Stop [1]. |
| **Pull Backward** | Move Backward [1]. |
| **Push Forward** | Move Forward [1]. |

*   For team files and exit procedures in zero-visibility conditions, see [[en/60_Advanced/Sidemount Guidelines & Operations in Overhead Environments|Sidemount Guidelines & Operations in Overhead Environments]] and [[en/50_Safety/Team Separation & Lost Diver SOP|Team Separation & Lost Diver SOP]].

## 🧭 Team Formations & Communication Flow

1.  **Pre-Dive Briefing Review**: Symmetrical review of signals before every dive. Ensure all divers share identical signal meanings, particularly when diving with buddies from different training agencies (PADI, GUE, and cave agencies have slight variations) [1][2].
2.  **Regular Pressure Reports**: The leader queries with a "?" hand sign and points to their gauge; divers report using the single-hand number system. In sidemount, report the **lower of the two cylinders** or follow team protocol to report both.
3.  **Parallel vs. Single File**: Swim parallel in open water (facilitating hand signals); swim in single file along a guideline (relying on light signals). The leader signals formation changes using light signals.
4.  **Debriefing Review**: Review any communication gaps or misunderstandings during the debrief, which is key to building team trust and efficiency [1].
