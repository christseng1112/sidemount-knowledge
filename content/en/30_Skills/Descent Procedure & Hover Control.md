# Descent Procedure & Hover Control (Descent Procedure & Hovering)

Descent is the phase where buoyancy changes most rapidly: exposure suits compress, wing bladders vent, and the full negative weight of dual cylinders is felt. A sidemount diver must manage venting, equalization, leak checks, and Trim during this phase. Hovering is the foundation of all sidemount skills—without a stable hover, you cannot safely execute valve drills or regulator switches in the water.

---

## ⬇ Standard Descent Procedure

### 1. Surface Preparation (Surface Checks)
*   Ensure cylinders are mounted correctly, bungees are in place, and hoses are free of wraps; perform buddy check (see [[en/70_Resources/Pre-Dive Checklists|Pre-Dive Checklists]]).
*   Breathe from the right-side long-hose regulator, confirming it is functioning; secure your mask.
*   Once descent hand signals are exchanged, **deflate and descend simultaneously**.

### 2. Deflate & Leave Surface
*   **Horizontal descent** is standard practice in sidemount: maintain a horizontal Trim and slowly vent your wing BCD, allowing the negative weight of the cylinders to carry you down [1].
*   Advantages of a horizontal descent: you maintain continuous eye contact with your buddy and the environment below, you can halt the descent instantly by taking a breath and adding gas to your wing, and you do not stir up silt upon landing.
*   Head-first descents are reserved for high-current open-water scenarios where a fast descent is required, and are not needed in recreational sidemount.

### 3. During Descent
1.  **Equalization**: Equalize your ears and mask early and often (low-volume masks require minimal gas to equalize, see [[en/20_Equipment/Fins and Mask Selection|Fins and Mask Selection]]).
2.  **Bubble Check**: Pause at 3–6 meters for a mutual bubble check, verifying that first stages, cylinder valves, and hose connections are leak-free [1].
3.  **Venting and Adding Gas**: Add small bursts of gas to your BCD every 3–5 meters to compensate for wetsuit compression and rising negative buoyancy. **Do not wait until you hit the bottom to add gas**.

### 4. Arriving at Target Depth
*   Begin establishing neutral buoyancy 2–3 meters before reaching target depth. Establish a stable hover, perform a **Flow Check** (verify that both cylinder valves are fully open), check your pressure gauges (SPGs), and begin your swim.

## 🧘 Hover Control (Hovering)

### Evaluation Standards
SDI/TDI sidemount course standards require: **maintaining a stable, motionless hover at a fixed depth without using hands or feet, and maintaining posture control throughout descent, bottom phase, and ascent** [2].

### Levels of Control (Coarse to Fine)

| Level | Tool | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Coarse | BCD Wing Inflation/Deflation | Depth adjustments greater than 1 meter. |
| Moderate | Lung Volume (Depth of Breath) | Positioning within ±0.5 meters. |
| Fine | Breathing Rhythm & Delay | Utilizing the latency of breath buoyancy to offset minor drift. |

*   **Key Principle**: Use the BCD to establish a baseline where your breathing midpoint yields neutral buoyancy, then rely on lung volume for fine control. If you find yourself gasping or holding deep breaths, your BCD volume is incorrect; return to coarse adjustment.
*   **Sidemount-Specific Variables**: As cylinder pressure decreases, cylinders become lighter (aluminum bottoms become positive). Your neutral buoyancy point will drift every 20–30 minutes, requiring minor BCD adjustments. Sizable pressure differences between the left and right tanks can also cause lateral roll (see [[en/40_Gas_Management/Sidemount Gas Switching Procedures|Sidemount Regulator Switching Procedure]] and [[en/30_Skills/In-Water Buoyancy & Horizontal Trim|In-Water Buoyancy & Horizontal Trim]]).

### Practice Progression
1.  Establish a stable hover facing a line or wall for 3 minutes $\rightarrow$ 2. Clear mask and perform regulator switches in a hover $\rightarrow$ 3. Perform full valve drills in a hover (see [[en/50_Safety/Valve Drills Standard Operating Procedures|Valve Drills Standard Operating Procedures]]) $\rightarrow$ 4. Hover 30 cm off the bottom without stirring up silt.
