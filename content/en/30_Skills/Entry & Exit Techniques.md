# Entry & Exit Techniques (Water Entry & Exit Techniques)

One of the greatest logistical advantages of sidemount diving is that **cylinders can be handled separately from the diver during entries and exits**. Utilizing this capability makes getting in and out of the water much easier than backmount configurations. Conversely, executing a giant stride with both cylinders mounted without using this advantage can be far more cumbersome than backmount. This guide outlines standard entry and exit procedures for boat and shore diving.

---

## 🚤 Boat Entries

### Method A: In-Water Clip-On (Highly Recommended & Ergonomic) [1][2]
1.  Enter the water wearing only your harness, fins, mask, and exposure suit (either via a giant stride or backward roll, carrying zero negative weight).
2.  The boat crew or your buddy **hands the cylinders to you one by one**: clip the left cylinder first (lower bolt snap to the hip D-ring, neck bungee wrapped around the valve), then secure the right cylinder.
3.  Organize hoses on the surface, perform a mutual check, and descend.
*   **Best Suited For**: Divers with back injuries, smaller divers, calm seas, and cooperative boat crews.
*   **Briefing Note**: If the crew is unfamiliar with sidemount, **brief the captain and deckhands on the handoff sequence** (valve orientation, which cylinder to hand first) before the dive.

### Method B: Single-Cylinder Giant Stride (The Hybrid Method) [1]
1.  On the deck, clip on the **left cylinder** (the neck snap can be temporarily clipped to the shoulder D-ring).
2.  **Hold the right cylinder in your hand** by the neck, secure your mask and left-side hoses, and step off (Giant Stride).
3.  Once in the water, clip the right cylinder onto your harness on the surface.
*   **Best Suited For**: High boat decks or rough seas where staying on the surface next to the boat is hazardous. Holding the cylinder in your hand minimizes the impact on the harness and bungees upon entry.

### Method C: Fully Rigged Giant Stride (Least Preferred)
*   Stepping off the deck with both cylinders fully rigged. The entry impact exerts a violent pull on the bungees and bolt snaps, which can rip hoses free. **This is not recommended from high boat decks**, and should only be used from low-to-water platforms or short drop heights.

### Boat Exits
*   **Unclip cylinders before climbing the ladder**: Unclip your cylinders one by one and hand them to the boat crew (or clip them to a gear line hung along the side of the boat) to climb the ladder weight-free [2].
*   In rough seas, keep the left cylinder clipped on (retaining a breathing gas source until fully out of the water) and hand the right cylinder up first.

## 🏖 Shore Entries & Exits

### Entries
1.  **Stage gear at the water's edge, carry harness separately**: Walk your cylinders to the shoreline first, then put on your harness, avoiding walking down steep or slippery gravel paths carrying full gear.
2.  **Clip on cylinders at waist depth**: Let the buoyancy of the water support the weight of the cylinders as you clip them on [1].
3.  **Surf Zone Management**: **Clip your cylinders on outside the surf zone**—attempting to bend over and clip gear inside breaking waves is a major tipping hazard. If the swell is heavy, move further out to chest-deep water (the limit of standing stability) to finish rigging.

### Exits
*   Reverse the entry process: walk into waist-deep water $\rightarrow$ unclip cylinders and carry or drag them to dry land $\rightarrow$ walk out of the water harness-only.
*   For long shallow flats, cylinders can be kept in a "towing position" inside their bungees and floated out, which is less tiring than carrying them by hand.

---

## ⚠️ Universal Principles

1.  **Breathe from your regulator before entry**: Whichever entry method you use, ensure your regulator is in your mouth and functioning before hitting the water.
2.  **Protect your mask and hoses**: When stepping off or rolling, use one hand to secure your mask and hoses, and the other to support the hand-held cylinder.
3.  **Standardize your surface clip-on sequence**: Always clip the same side first (standard: left first, then right, since the left cylinder typically provides inflation gas for the BCD/drysuit) to establish muscle memory.
4.  **Perform a surface check after rigging**: Verify that bungees are seated, clips are attached to the correct D-rings, and the long hose can be fully deployed—**any alignment issues on the surface will worsen underwater** (see [[en/70_Resources/Pre-Dive Checklists|Pre-Dive Checklists]] and [[en/50_Safety/Bungee Failure & Cylinder Dislodgement Procedures|Neck Bungee Failure & Lost Cylinder Handling]]).
