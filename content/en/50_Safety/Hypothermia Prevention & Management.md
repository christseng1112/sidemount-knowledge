# Hypothermia Prevention & Management (Hypothermia Prevention & Management)

Water conducts heat over 20 times faster than air, making hypothermia one of the most underestimated risks in cold-water diving. Beyond physical discomfort, cold exposure **impairs judgment, increases DCS risk, and reduces manual dexterity**, directly impacting a sidemount diver's ability to operate bolt snaps and cylinder valves.

---

## 🌡 Definitions and Thermal Thresholds

*   **Hypothermia**: Defined as a drop in core body temperature below **35°C (95°F)** [1][2].
*   **Water Temperature Guide**: Most divers benefit from thermal protection in water below **27°C (80°F)**. Water below **24°C (75°F)** presents significant thermal stress [2].
*   Hypothermia does not require freezing water—**cumulative heat loss over multi-dive tropical itineraries** (e.g., 27°C water, thin wetsuit, four dives a day) can result in mild hypothermia.

---

## 📉 Symptom Classifications [1][2]

| Stage | Symptoms | Field Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mild** | Shivering, cold skin, mild coordination loss, frequent urination. | **Terminate the dive**—shivering is your body's abort signal. |
| **Moderate** | Continuous shivering, loss of manual dexterity, mental slowness, elevated heart rate. | Exit the water immediately and begin rewarming. |
| **Severe** | **Shivering stops**, apathy, slurred speech, confusion, loss of consciousness. | Medical emergency—handle gently and evacuate. |

> ⚠️ **"Shivering stops" is a sign of deterioration, not recovery**: When shivering stops, the body has exhausted its ability to generate heat, indicating onset of severe hypothermia [1].

### In-Water Behavioral Symptoms
Cognitive decline often precedes the sensation of cold: slow movement, delayed responses to signals, and simple errors in gear operation (difficulty clipping bolt snaps, wrong gas switches). **Teammates must actively monitor each other; self-assessment is unreliable.**

---

## 🧯 Field Response SOP [1][2]

1.  **Controlled Exit**: Do not rush. Maintain standard ascent rates and execute safety stops (unless severe hypothermia is present) (see [[en/50_Safety/Ascent Rate & Safety Stop Standards|Ascent Rate & Safety Stop Standards]]).
2.  **Dry and Shelter**: Immediately remove wet exposure suits, dry the skin, dress in warm clothing, and shelter from wind chill.
3.  **Mild to Moderate Care**: Offer warm, non-alcoholic beverages to conscious, swallowing-safe casualties (**avoid alcohol**—vasodilation increases core heat loss; caffeine is also contraindicated) [1]. Apply blankets to the chest and core rather than extremities. Shivering is an effective rewarming mechanism; do not restrict it [1].
4.  **Severe Care**: Handle with **extreme gentleness** (cold myocardium is highly susceptible to ventricular fibrillation). Keep the casualty horizontal, do not massage extremities, withhold oral fluids, and evacuate immediately.
5.  **DCS Risk Interaction**: Cold exposure causes peripheral vasoconstriction, reducing gas elimination efficiency. Treat any post-dive DCS symptoms in cold water conservatively (see [[en/50_Safety/DCS Symptom Recognition & Oxygen First Aid|DCS Symptom Recognition & Oxygen First Aid]]).

## 🛡 Sidemount Prevention Checklist

1.  **Select Exposure Suits for the Coldest Point**: Base suit thickness on bottom temperatures rather than surface values (see [[en/20_Equipment/Exposure Suits Selection & Buoyancy Trim Impact|Exposure Suits Selection & Buoyancy Trim Impact]]). Drysuit undergarment layering dictates overall thermal safety.
2.  **Ensure Surface Rewarming**: Drink warm fluids and change into dry clothes during surface intervals. A warm skin temperature does not mean your core temperature has recovered.
3.  **Maintain Manual Dexterity**: If water temperatures are below 15°C, use dry gloves or three-finger mitts. **Practice operating bolt snaps, cylinder valves, and spools wearing thick gloves on land**. If you cannot manage your gear, your safety margin is compromised (for signal adjustments, see [[en/30_Skills/Team Hand Signals, Light & Touch Communication|Team Hand Signals, Light & Touch Communication]]).
4.  **Establish Abort Standards**: Agree in your pre-dive briefing that if any team member begins to shiver, the team terminates the dive.
