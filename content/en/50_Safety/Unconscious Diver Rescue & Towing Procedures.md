# Unconscious Diver Rescue & Towing Procedures (Unconscious Diver Rescue & Tow)

> ⚠️ **This article is intended for review by divers certified in rescue diving (Rescue Diver or equivalent).** Rescue skills must be practiced in the water under instructor guidance; text descriptions cannot replace hands-on training.

The physical design of sidemount equipment makes the gear removal phase of a rescue different from backmount: **there is no single BCD jacket to slide off; instead, you manage two independent cylinders that must be unclipped**. This article covers standard rescue protocols, highlighting sidemount-specific modifications.

---

## 🌊 Underwater Management of an Unresponsive Diver

1.  **Verify Responsiveness**: Shake the diver's shoulders, look for exhaled bubbles, and check for eye movement.
2.  **Control Ascent**: Position yourself behind the diver. Use one hand to keep their head tilted back (maintaining an open airway) and use your other hand to operate **their BCD exhaust valve** to control the ascent rate [1].
3.  **Regulator Management**: If the regulator is in the diver's mouth, hold it in place. **If it has fallen out, do not try to re-insert it**. Consensus guidelines dictate that attempting to insert a regulator into an unconscious airway carries a high risk of forcing water into the lungs [1].
4.  **Ascent Rate**: Keep the ascent rate within standard limits (see [[en/50_Safety/Ascent Rate & Safety Stop Standards|Ascent Rate & Safety Stop Standards]]). Skip safety stops if the diver is not breathing—**drowning management takes precedence over DCS risk**.
5.  **Sidemount Grab Points**: When managing a sidemount casualty, grab their harness shoulder webbing or the cylinder necks. Do not pull on their neck bungees, as this can dislodge the cylinders (see [[en/50_Safety/Bungee Failure & Cylinder Dislodgement Procedures|Bungee Failure & Cylinder Dislodgement Procedures]]).

## 🌅 Surface Management SOP [1][2]

1.  **Roll Face Up & Establish Buoyancy**: Turn the casualty face up. **Inflate their BCD and drop their weights**, while verifying your own positive buoyancy [1].
    *   **Sidemount Weight Systems**: Sidemount ballast is typically located along the spine pocket or harness trim pockets (see [[en/20_Equipment/Exposure Suit Selection & Trim Influence|Exposure Suits Selection & Buoyancy Trim Impact]]). Make sure you know where your buddy's weights are located and how to release them.
2.  **Verify Breathing**: Remove their mask, open their airway, and check for breathing for 5–10 seconds.
3.  **No Breathing $\rightarrow$ Deliver Rescue Breaths**: Administer **2 initial breaths**, followed by **1 breath every 5 seconds** (mouth-to-mask or mouth-to-nose) [1][2]. If breathing is questionable, always deliver rescue breaths; the risk to a breathing casualty is minimal, whereas withholding breaths from a non-breathing casualty is fatal [2].
4.  **De-rig While Towing**: Coordinate towing with rescue breathing, prioritizing breathing checks over gear removal [1].
    *   **Sidemount De-rigging Order**: Unclip the casualty's **far-side cylinder** (slide bungee off the valve $\rightarrow$ unclip the lower bolt snap $\rightarrow$ push the cylinder away or let it sink) $\rightarrow$ repeat on the near-side cylinder $\rightarrow$ unclip harness buckles. Each tank has only two attachment points and can be removed single-handedly.
    *   **Rescuer Gear**: Remove your own cylinders if they impede your towing speed.
5.  **Extraction**: Continue rescue breathing until the casualty is ready to be lifted out of the water (ladder assist or shore drag) [1].
6.  **Post-Extraction**: Initiate CPR and deploy an AED $\rightarrow$ administer 100% emergency oxygen $\rightarrow$ contact emergency medical services and DAN (see [[en/50_Safety/DCS Symptom Recognition & Oxygen First Aid|DCS Symptom Recognition & Oxygen First Aid]]).

## 🚣 Towing Techniques

| Towing Style | Execution | Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Modified Do-Si-Do** | Reach under casualty's armpit to cup their chin, keeping airway open | Primary towing method while delivering rescue breaths. |
| **Valve/Harness Tow** | Grasp the casualty's cylinder valve or shoulder harness | High-speed towing over distances when rescue breathing is paused. |
| **Two-Rescuer Tow** | One rescuer manages the airway/breaths; the second swims the casualty | Most efficient method when a second rescuer is present. |

*   Towing a casualty over long distances is physically exhausting (refer to high workload RMVs in [[en/40_Gas_Management/Gas Consumption Rate (SAC & RMV) Calculation|Gas Consumption Rate (SAC & RMV) Calculation]]). **Always call for surface or boat support before starting the tow**.

## 🛡 Prevention & Preparation

*   Common causes of unconsciousness include hypoxia, hypercapnia, cardiac events, and severe nitrogen narcosis. For technical prevention, see [[en/40_Gas_Management/Oxygen Exposure & Nitrogen Narcosis Management|Oxygen Exposure & Nitrogen Narcosis Management]] and [[en/50_Safety/Hypothermia Prevention & Management|Hypothermia Prevention & Management]].
*   **Practice Sidemount Rescue Drills**: The gear removal steps learned in standard backmount rescue courses must be adapted to sidemount configurations—specifically weight drops and independent cylinder unclipping.
