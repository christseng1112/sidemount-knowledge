# Bungee Failure & Cylinder Dislodgement Procedures (Bungee Failure & Lost Cylinder)

Two equipment failures unique to sidemount diving—**sidemount neck bungee failure** and **cylinder dislodgement (lost cylinder)**—receive little coverage in standard recreational manuals. Both failures cause the cylinder to detach from the diver's torso, disrupting horizontal Trim and increasing the risk of entanglements and gas loss. This article provides standard operating procedures (SOPs) for these emergencies.

> ⚠️ **Safety Notice**: This article is intended for review by certified sidemount divers. Always practice skills under the guidance of a qualified instructor and your training agency's standards.

---

## 🔗 1. Sidemount Neck Bungee Failure

In sidemount configurations, the **cylinder neck/valve is drawn into the armpit using an elastic bungee**, while the cylinder bottom is clipped to a waist or hip D-ring. If the bungee snaps or slips off the valve handwheel underwater:

### Symptoms
*   The valve end of the cylinder loses its upper attachment point, causing the **cylinder neck to swing outward and downward (pendulum effect)**. The tank remains connected only at the hip.
*   Physical drag increases, horizontal Trim is thrown out of balance, and the exposed regulator first stage and valve handwheel are vulnerable to ceiling impacts or line entanglements.

### 🟢 Emergency SOP
1.  **Stabilize Buoyancy & Trim**: Establish neutral buoyancy to prevent a runaway ascent or colliding with overhead walls.
2.  **Control the Cylinder Neck**: Grasp the cylinder valve or first stage with one hand, pull the cylinder back into your armpit, and secure it by clamping your upper arm flat against your ribcage.
3.  **Deploy Backup Bungee**: Retrieve a **spare sidemount bungee** (it is recommended to carry a pre-cut spare bungee in your harness utility pocket; see [[en/20_Equipment/Razor Sidemount System Adjustment|Razor Sidemount System Adjustment]]) and hook it onto the valve handwheel and shoulder D-ring.
4.  **Alternative Rigging (If no spare bungee is available)**: Clip the cylinder neck bolt snap **directly to your shoulder D-ring** as a temporary hard connection, or unclip the lower snap and hold the cylinder in front of your chest in a No-mount carry (see [[en/30_Skills/In-Water Cylinder Management Techniques|In-Water Cylinder Management Techniques]]).
5.  **Signal Buddy & Terminate Dive**: In overhead environments, follow the guideline to exit; in open water, conduct a controlled ascent to the surface.

### 🛡 Prevention
*   Bungee cord is a **consumable item**. Inspect it regularly and replace it immediately if cracks or dry rot appear (see [[en/20_Equipment/Sidemount Gear Maintenance & Care Guide|Sidemount Gear Maintenance & Care Guide]]).
*   Verify that the bungee is looped securely around the valve handwheel during your pre-dive checks (the "B" in the BAR check; see [[en/70_Resources/Pre-Dive Checklists|Pre-Dive Checklists]]).

## 🆘 2. Cylinder Dislodgement (Lost or Dropped Cylinder)

If a lower bolt snap fails or you drop a cylinder during dynamic manipulation (such as unclipping, pushing, or no-mount exploration), the cylinder can drift away.

### 🟢 Open Water SOP
1.  **Retrieve if Possible**: If the cylinder is within reach, grasp the valve, secure the neck bungee first, and then clip the lower bolt snap to restore your configuration.
2.  **Do Not Chase a Deep Tank**: If the cylinder sinks past your planned depth or gas limits, **do not chase it**. Switch to your remaining active cylinder, verify that your remaining gas meets your Rock Bottom reserve requirements (see [[en/40_Gas_Management/Gas Planning & Safety Reserve Principles|Gas Planning & Safety Reserve Principles]]), and execute a controlled ascent to terminate the dive.
3.  **Manage Asymmetrical Trim**: Losing a cylinder means **losing half your gas supply and half your lateral ballast**. Adjust your core muscles and cant your fins to compensate for the weight offset (refer to [[en/30_Skills/Single Cylinder Sidemount Configuration|Single Cylinder Sidemount Configuration]]).

### 🔴 Overhead Environment SOP (Caves & Wrecks)
*   Dropping a cylinder in an overhead environment is a **critical emergency**. Decompression and exit gas calculations assume a two-cylinder supply; losing a tank can directly compromise your exit gas reserve.
*   Signal your team immediately, verify your connection to the guideline, and initiate an exit using your remaining gas and your buddy's long hose if necessary, following your team's gas limits.

### 🛡 Prevention
*   Always loop a **wrist lanyard** over the cylinder valve before unclipping a tank to push it ahead of you (see [[en/60_Advanced/No-Mount & Stage Dropping Techniques|No-Mount & Stage Dropping Techniques]]).
*   Use **large-eye double-ended or single bolt snaps** and check spring tension regularly. Replace sticky or weak snaps (see [[en/20_Equipment/Cylinder Rigging and Hardware Setup|Cylinder Rigging and Hardware Setup]]).
*   Verify that the lower bolt snaps are securely clipped to their D-rings during your pre-dive checks (the "C" in the BAR check).
